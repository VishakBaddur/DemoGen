\paragraph{Name-level variance check.}
Because demographic groups are represented through names, one threat to validity is that measured differences may reflect particular name choices rather than a broader group-level effect. To partially assess this, we computed the variance of mean LLM quality scores across names within each perceived racial group. In the Baseline condition, within-race name-mean variance was non-trivial: 0.0079 for Asian-associated names, 0.0184 for Black-associated names, and 0.0072 for White-associated names, compared with a between-race mean variance of 0.0035. This indicates that individual name effects are present and sometimes larger than the aggregate race-level mean difference. After Persona-Blind prompting, all absolute name-level variances became very small (approximately 0.0006--0.0010), consistent with the idea that removing names reduces both group-level and name-specific variation. We therefore interpret our race-level results as a controlled name-based audit of perceived demographic cues, while noting that future work should use larger and more systematically balanced name sets to separate name-specific and group-level effects more cleanly.
