\paragraph{Explicit Asian--Black comparison.}
Because the largest Baseline quality gap occurs between Asian-associated and Black-associated names, we also report this pair directly. In the Baseline condition, Asian-associated prompts had a mean LLM quality score of 4.804, while Black-associated prompts had a mean of 4.694, giving an absolute gap of 0.110. A Welch two-sample t-test for this pair gives $p=0.016$ with Cohen's $d=0.255$ (Asian minus Black), indicating a small but statistically detectable pairwise difference for the primary quality metric. Under Persona-Blind prompting, the same Asian--Black quality gap drops to 0.017 ($p=0.254$), and under Reranked generation it is 0.063 ($p=0.132$).
